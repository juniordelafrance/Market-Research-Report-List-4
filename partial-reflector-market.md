<p><p>Partial reflectors are used in various applications, with three main types dominating the market. Plane partial reflectors are flat surfaces that reflect a portion of the incident light. Concave partial reflectors are curved inward, focusing the reflected light towards a specific point. On the other hand, convex partial reflectors are curved outward, dispersing the reflected light over a wider area. Each type has its unique advantages and is chosen based on the specific requirements of the application.</p></p>
