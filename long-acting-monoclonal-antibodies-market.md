<p><p>Long-acting monoclonal antibodies are classified based on their source into three types: murine source, chimeric source, and human source. Murine source antibodies are derived from mice, chimeric source antibodies are a combination of mouse and human proteins, and human source antibodies are fully human in origin. These different types of antibodies have varying levels of immunogenicity and efficacy, with human source antibodies generally being preferred due to lower risk of adverse reactions and improved therapeutic benefits.</p></p>
