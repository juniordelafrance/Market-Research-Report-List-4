<p><ul><li>Polyvinylidene Fluoride</li><li>Polytetrafluoroethylene</li><li>Perfluoroethylene Propylene Copolymer</li><li>Ethylene-Tetrafluoroethylene Copolymer</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fluoropolymer coating market consists of several key types, each with unique properties and applications. Polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) is known for its durability and chemical resistance, making it suitable for architectural coatings. Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) is famous for its non-stick properties and high-temperature resistance, ideal for cookware and industrial applications. Perfluoroethylene propylene copolymer (FEP) offers excellent chemical resistance and transparency for wiring insulation. Ethylene-tetrafluoroethylene copolymer (ETFE) combines flexibility and strength for various uses, while "Other" encompasses specialized fluoropolymers with tailored characteristics.</p></p>
