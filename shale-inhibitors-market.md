<p><p>Shale inhibitors are chemicals added to drilling fluids to prevent the swelling and disintegration of shale formations during drilling operations. There are four main types of shale inhibitors on the market: plaster, silicate, lime, and potassium salt. Plaster inhibitors work by forming a protective film on the shale surface, while silicate inhibitors create a barrier that prevents shale hydration. Lime inhibitors neutralize acidic components in the drilling fluid, and potassium salt inhibitors help control shale swelling by exchanging ions with the shale particles.</p></p>
