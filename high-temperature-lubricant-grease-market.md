<p><p>High temperature lubricants and greases can be classified into two main types based on their thickener composition: soap thickener and non-soap thickener. Soap thickened lubricants use metallic soaps as thickeners, such as lithium, aluminum, or calcium soaps. On the other hand, non-soap thickened lubricants use alternative thickeners like polyurea, complex lithium, or clay. Each type of thickener offers unique properties and advantages, catering to different applications and environments where high temperatures are a concern.</p></p>
