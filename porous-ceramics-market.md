<p><p>Porous ceramics consist of two main types in the market: oxides ceramics and non-oxides ceramics. Oxides ceramics are composed of compounds like alumina, zirconia, and silica, which offer high chemical stability and thermal resistance. On the other hand, non-oxides ceramics, such as silicon carbide and boron nitride, provide superior mechanical properties and wear resistance. Both types of porous ceramics are widely used in various industries for applications like filtration, catalysis, insulation, and structural components.</p></p>
