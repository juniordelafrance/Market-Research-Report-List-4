<p><p>Electronic faucets are becoming increasingly popular in the market, with two main types. Touchless electronic faucets, also known as sensor faucets, use motion sensors to detect when hands are placed under the faucet and automatically dispense water. Touched electronic faucets, on the other hand, require physical contact to activate the water flow, typically through a lever or button. Both types offer the benefits of water conservation, hygiene, and convenience, making them attractive options for residential and commercial settings.</p></p>
