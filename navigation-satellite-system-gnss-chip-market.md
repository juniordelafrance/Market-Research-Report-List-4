<p><p>The Navigation Satellite System (GNSS) Chip market can be divided into two main types: High Precision and Standard Precision. High Precision GNSS chips are designed for applications that require extremely accurate positioning, such as surveying, mapping, and precision agriculture. These chips offer centimeter-level accuracy and are generally more expensive. On the other hand, Standard Precision GNSS chips provide meter-level accuracy and are suitable for more general navigation and location-based services. They are typically more cost-effective and widely used in consumer devices like smartphones and automotive navigation systems.</p></p>
