<p><p>The pediatric extracorporeal blood pumps market includes several types that cater to specific medical needs. Roller pumps use a mechanism to compress tubing, delivering consistent flow rates. Centrifugal pumps rely on rotational force for blood movement, ensuring smooth operation. Pulsatile pumps mimic the natural heartbeat, providing tailored support in critical situations. Non-occlusive pumps minimize vessel trauma and maintain blood vessel patency. Together, these technologies enhance pediatric care by supporting surgeries and treatments requiring blood circulation management.</p></p>
