<p><p>Medical lasers are categorized based on the type of technology used in their design. Solid-state laser systems, which use a solid medium to generate the laser beam, are commonly used for surgical procedures. Gas laser systems, on the other hand, utilize gas as the active medium and are preferred for dermatological treatments. Dye laser systems incorporate a liquid dye as the medium, suitable for aesthetic procedures. Diode laser systems, using diode as the medium, are versatile and widely utilized in various medical applications due to their compact size and efficiency.</p></p>
