<p><ul><li>Drylaid</li><li>Airlaid</li><li>Wetlaid</li><li>Spunlaid</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Medical hygiene non-woven fabric market types include drylaid, airlaid, wetlaid, and spunlaid. Drylaid fabrics are produced by laying fibers on a conveyer belt and binding them together with heat, pressure, or chemicals. Airlaid fabrics involve a process where fibers are dispersed in the air and then bonded together. Wetlaid fabrics are made by dispersing fibers in water and forming a sheet through a filtration process. Spunlaid fabrics are created by extruding fibers onto a conveyor belt and bonding them together.</p></p>
