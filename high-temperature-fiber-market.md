<p><p>High-temperature fibers are divided into various types including Basalt, Aramid, Ceramic, and others. Basalt fibers are made from natural volcanic rock and exhibit high strength and durability. Aramid fibers, such as Kevlar, are known for their exceptional heat resistance and strength. Ceramic fibers are made from inorganic materials and are commonly used in high-temperature applications. Other types of high-temperature fibers may include carbon, quartz, and silicon carbide fibers. Each type offers unique properties and benefits for use in demanding high-temperature environments.</p></p>
