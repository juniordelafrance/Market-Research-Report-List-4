<p><p>Allograft Prostheses are ligament replacements made from donor tissues, usually taken from cadavers. These prostheses are known for their biocompatibility and ability to integrate with the recipient's body. Synthetic Prostheses, on the other hand, are made from artificial materials such as polyester or polyethylene. They are designed to mimic the function of natural ligaments and are often used when allografts are not available or not suitable for the patient. Both types of prostheses play a crucial role in restoring joint function and stability in patients with ligament injuries.</p></p>
