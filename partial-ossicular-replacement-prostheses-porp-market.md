<p><p>Partial Ossicular Replacement Prostheses (PORP) are surgical implants used in middle ear surgeries to replace one or more ossicles when there is conductive hearing loss due to middle ear conditions like cholesteatoma or otosclerosis. These prostheses help in restoring sound transmission by connecting the remaining ossicular chain to the oval window of the cochlea.</p><p>The PORP market is witnessing significant growth, driven by an increase in ear-related diseases and advancements in surgical techniques. As more people seek solutions for hearing impairments, the demand for effective interventions, including PORPs, has surged. The aging population, which is more susceptible to hearing loss, further contributes to market expansion.</p><p>Emerging trends include the development of bio-compatible materials and novel designs that enhance the prostheses' performance and patient outcomes.
