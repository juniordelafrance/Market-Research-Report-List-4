<p><ul><li>Anionic surfactants</li><li>Non-ionic surfactants</li><li>Cationic surfactants</li><li>Amphoteric surfactants</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The surface-active substances market comprises various types of surfactants, each distinguished by their ionic properties. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge, making them effective in cleaning and emulsifying. Non-ionic surfactants have no charge, offering mildness and stability in sensitive applications. Cationic surfactants are positively charged, commonly used in fabric softeners and disinfectants. Amphoteric surfactants can adopt either charge depending on the environment, making them versatile. Additionally, "Others" includes specialized surfactants used for niche applications, enhancing product performance in various industries.</p></p>
