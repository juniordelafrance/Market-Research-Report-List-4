<p><p>Dry chickpeas are divided into two main market types: Kabuli chickpeas and Desi chickpeas. Kabuli chickpeas are larger in size, lighter in color, and have a smooth coat. They are commonly used in salads and other dishes for their mild flavor. Desi chickpeas, on the other hand, are smaller, darker in color, and have a thicker coat. They are more commonly used in Indian and Middle Eastern cuisines for their nutty and earthy flavor. Both types of chickpeas are popular in various global markets for their versatility and nutritional benefits.</p></p>
