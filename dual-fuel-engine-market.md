<p><p>The dual fuel engine market can be categorized based on the type of engine cycle used: four-stroke and two-stroke. Four-stroke engines are characterized by four piston strokes required to complete a single cycle (intake, compression, power, and exhaust). On the other hand, two-stroke engines complete a cycle with only two piston strokes (compression and power). Each market type offers distinct advantages in terms of efficiency, power output, and emissions control, catering to different industry needs and applications.</p></p>
