<p><p>A Metal Oxide Disc Varistor (MOV) is a type of voltage-dependent resistor primarily used for surge protection in electrical circuits. By providing a low-resistance path during voltage spikes, MOVs protect sensitive components from transient overvoltages. Their robust design features a ceramic disc composed of zinc oxide and other metal oxides, which enables them to effectively clamp and stabilize voltage levels.</p><p>The Metal Oxide Disc Varistor Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.60% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing demand for consumer electronics, energy systems, and electric vehicles, where reliable overvoltage protection is critical. The rise of renewable energy sources has also spurred interest in MOVs for safeguarding inverters and other electronic systems against electrical surges. Furthermore, emerging markets are witnessing rapid industrialization, leading to more installations of MOVs in various applications, including telecommunications and industrial machinery.</p><p>Latest trends indicate a shift towards miniaturization and enhanced performance customization, as manufacturers aim to meet the precise needs of modern electronics. Innovations in materials and design are enabling the creation of more efficient and durable MOVs, positioning them as essential components in a wide array of applications and enhancing their market prospects.</p></p>
