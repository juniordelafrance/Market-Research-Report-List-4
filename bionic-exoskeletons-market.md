<p><p>Bionic exoskeletons can be categorized into two main types: Active and Passive exoskeletons. Active exoskeletons are powered and assist the user's movements through electric motors, providing additional strength and support. On the other hand, Passive exoskeletons do not require external power and instead rely on mechanical structures and springs to support the wearer's movements. Both types of exoskeletons have their own unique applications and benefits in various industries such as healthcare, military, and manufacturing.</p></p>
