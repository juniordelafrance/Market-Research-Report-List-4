<p><p>Ambulatory cardiac monitors are devices used to record and monitor a patient's heart activity outside of a clinical setting. The different types of ambulatory cardiac monitors include Holter monitors, which continuously record heart activity over a period of 24-48 hours, event monitors, which are worn for longer durations and activated by the patient when symptoms occur, mobile cardiac telemetry devices, which transmit real-time data to healthcare providers, and other emerging technologies such as implantable loop recorders. Each type of monitor serves a unique purpose in the diagnosis and management of cardiac conditions.</p></p>
